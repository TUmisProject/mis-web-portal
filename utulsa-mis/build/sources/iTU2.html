Information Technologists United Overview

Information Technologists United, also known as iTU, is a professional organization for MIS majors and minors. Through peer to peer mentoring, professional events, and company presentations, iTU prepares undergraduate students for internships and jobs relating to MIS.

Membership Requirements

  • Pay dues of either $20 per year or $15 per semester
  • Maintain a minimum 2.5 GPA
  • Attend at least 3 iTU sponsored events per semester

Officer Job Description

President

As President, Abby is responsible for overseeing the iTU program as a whole, including coordinating and facilitating monthly professional meetings, helping officers plan events such as mentorship events and banquets, and serving as the liaison between students, MIS faculty, and IT professionals in the Tulsa/Oklahoma area wishing to come speak to MIS students. She makes sure each officer is fulfilling their respective duties and serves as a resource if any additional assistance is needed for operations and logistics in the organization to run smoothly.

Vice President

As Vice President, Rachael is responsible for assisting the President in coordinating monthly meetings and ordering food for executive meetings for the officers. Her primary responsibility is planning the iTU End of the Year Banquet, where closing remarks are made about the MIS program, seniors and outstanding IT TU alum are recognized, and officer installations for incoming officers take place.

Treasurer

As Treasurer, David is responsible for handling all finances of our organization. He ensures our budget is kept on track, dues are being collected by members, and reimbursements are taken care of properly and in a timely manner.

Secretary

As Secretary, Melanie is responsible for recording officer meeting minutes and send out after meeting complete. She creates sign-in sheets for each professional meeting to help us keep track of our members’ attendance. She also creates the digital signage to be displayed at different places throughout campus, as well as signage to be sent to all MIS majors and minors prior to our monthly professional meetings.

Mentorship Chair

This position is responsible for advertising and arranging iTU Mentorship events, pairing mentee sign-ups with mentors, and general recruitment of Management Information Systems majors and minors.

Junior Representative

As Junior Representative, Ryan is responsible for communication with the junior class about all things iTU. This involves encouraging attendance, getting people excited about events, and continuing to spread iTU throughout campus. Recruitment and communication are his primary jobs.

Senior Representative

As Senior Representative, Jocelyn is responsible for communication with the junior class about all things iTU. This involves encouraging attendance, getting people excited about events, and continuing to spread iTU throughout campus. Like junior representative, recruitment and communication are her primary jobs.
